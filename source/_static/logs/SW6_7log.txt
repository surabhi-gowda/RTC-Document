OSF = Oscillator Stop Flag

For RV-4162, it’s bit 7 of the seconds register (0x00) or bit 7 in 0x0F depending on exact chip.
Purpose: Indicates if the oscillator has stopped due to total power loss or crystal failure.

Meaning:
OSF = 1 → The RTC lost its time base. The clock data may be invalid and must be reset by the user.
OSF = 0 → Oscillator running fine.

Example from your dump:
-----------------------
Register 0x00: 97 → Binary: 1001 0111

  - Bit 7 (MSB) = 1 → OSF = 1
  - Lower bits: 001 0111 = 0x17 (23 seconds in BCD)

Result:
-------
   OSF is set → Confirms the oscillator stopped due to power loss or crystal failure.
   Time must be re-initialized and OSF cleared by writing back the seconds value with MSB = 0.

Example Clear Command:
  i2cset -f -y 1 0x68 0x00 0x17


Bit‑Level Access:
-----------------
Register 0x08 = Frequency Compensation Register

Step 1: Read initial value
Step 2: Write new value
Step 3: Modify again

Example:
--------
